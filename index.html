What is Anemia?

A silent condition affecting
1 in 3 people worldwide

Anemia happens when your body doesn't have enough healthy red blood cells to carry oxygen to your organs. It is the world's most prevalent nutritional disorder — yet most cases go undetected because symptoms like fatigue and breathlessness are easily dismissed as stress or poor sleep.

A simple Complete Blood Count (CBC) test is all it takes to detect it. HemoScan AI reads those four numbers — Hemoglobin, MCV, MCH and MCHC — and returns an instant AI-powered prediction, severity score, and personalised treatment plan.

Understanding Anemia

Types, Causes & Symptoms

Anemia is not one disease — it is a final common pathway of many different conditions. Knowing the type determines the treatment.

Types of Anemia
Iron-Deficiency Anemia
Most Common · ~50% of cases

Low iron means fewer, smaller red cells. Caused by poor diet, blood loss (menstruation, GI bleed) or poor absorption. The hallmark CBC pattern is low Hgb with low MCV and MCH.

↓ Hgb ↓ MCV <80 fL ↓ MCH <27 pg
Vitamin B12 / Folate Deficiency
Macrocytic · Megaloblastic

Impaired DNA synthesis produces oversized, immature red cells. Common in strict vegetarians, elderly patients, and those with malabsorption (Crohn's, celiac disease).

↓ Hgb ↑ MCV >100 fL Normal MCHC
Anemia of Chronic Disease
Normocytic · Inflammatory

Chronic illness (kidney disease, cancer, rheumatoid arthritis) triggers inflammation that suppresses red cell production. Cell size is normal but total count is reduced.

↓ Hgb Normal MCV Normal MCH
Hemolytic Anemia
Destruction-based · Variable

Red blood cells are destroyed faster than they are replaced. Can be inherited (sickle cell, thalassemia) or acquired (autoimmune, drug-induced, infection).

↓ Hgb ↑ Reticulocytes ↑ Bilirubin

Common Causes

  • Poor diet — insufficient iron, B12, or folate
  • Blood loss — heavy periods, GI bleeding, surgery
  • Chronic illness — kidney disease, cancer, HIV
  • Genetic conditions — sickle cell, thalassemia
  • Bone marrow failure — aplastic anemia, leukemia
  • Pregnancy — increased demand, haemodilution

Signs & Symptoms

Fatigue & weakness
Pale or yellow skin
Shortness of breath
Dizziness
Rapid heartbeat
Cold hands & feet

Chest pain
